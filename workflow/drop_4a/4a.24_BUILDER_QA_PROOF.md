## 3. Defense-in-Depth Inspection

The gate runs identical role-checking at two layers, which earns its keep:

- **Adapter** (`app_service_adapter_mcp.go:259-261`): `authSessionRoleMayGovernOthers(actingSession)` rejects non-orchestrators at the transport boundary using `ErrAuthorizationDenied`.
- **Service** (`auth_requests.go:424-426`): `session.PrincipalRole != "orchestrator"` rejects again with `ErrAuthorizationDenied` — this catches in-process callers that bypass the adapter (e.g. test fixtures invoking `s.ApproveAuthRequest` directly), and also catches the case where the adapter was bypassed at any reason.

The session-vs-input cross-check at `auth_requests.go:431-433` (Reject 4) prevents the documented identity-spoof attack: a caller could otherwise submit `ApproverPrincipalID = "ORCH_FAMOUS"` while their actual session belongs to `ORCH_OBSCURE`, effectively dressing up their gate decision in someone else's identity. The cross-check forces session-`PrincipalID` and supplied-`ApproverPrincipalID` to match, closing the surface.

The `requireStewardPersistentAncestor` walk uses a visited-set cycle guard (`auth_requests.go:495-499`) — defensive against pathological action-item parent-cycles in storage. `errors.Is(err, ErrNotFound)` short-circuits the walk cleanly when an ancestor is missing rather than propagating unrelated repo errors.
